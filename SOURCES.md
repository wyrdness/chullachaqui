## Research Principles

### Respect for Indigenous Knowledge

When researching Chullachaqui:

1. **Prioritize Indigenous Voices**: Indigenous communities are the primary authorities
2. **Proper Consultation**: Always consult with communities before research
3. **Reciprocity**: Give back to communities (share findings, support initiatives)
4. **Context**: Understand traditional ecological knowledge context
5. **Not Superstition**: Recognize practical wisdom, not mere belief
6. **Cultural Property**: Respect intellectual and cultural property rights
7. **Ongoing Permission**: Maintain relationships and ongoing consent

### Traditional Ecological Knowledge

Chullachaqui is part of traditional ecological knowledge systems that:
- Have proven effective over centuries
- Integrate spiritual and practical knowledge
- Maintain biodiversity and forest health
- Teach sustainable resource use
- Protect sacred and ecologically important areas
- Pass knowledge through generations

### Academic Ethics

Researchers should:
- Follow ethical guidelines for indigenous research
- Obtain proper permissions and consents
- Share benefits of research with communities
- Avoid extractive or exploitative approaches
- Support indigenous cultural preservation
- Recognize indigenous intellectual property
